About the Chef

Calling a person who has never had a cooking job, let alone a head chef job, a chef is a bit of a misnomer. In most circles,
I would merely consider myself a home cook, and a very amateur one at that. I grew up in Harford County, Maryland, which
as anyone could tell you is technically part of the southern states. It does after all sit below the mason-dixon line,
which historically divided the northern and southern states. However, if you've lived in that area, you probably consider
yourself part of the northern states, and you'd mostly be right. I moved to Charlotte, North Carolina in 2012, and everyone
considers North Carolina a southern state. So here I am, a "Yankee" living in the "South." The cooking I do typically is
inspired by my childhood, in which my father was always the chef in the house. Yankee Southern Cooking is a way for me to
show how where I was born inspires how I cook now.

Some of the recipes I've written about are adapted from other well-known chefs. If so, I give credit where it's due, and I'll
post the original recipe alongside my own. Others are family recipes, or recipes that my father adapted to his own style, and
I've since adapted myself. I don't have any attributable sources for those other than my family, and so they're what I'll
consider original recipes. Feel free to use them however you'd like. Maybe you'll find a better way to do something.
If you do, let me know! Shoot me an e-mail!

If you'd like to see how I do cooking one of your recipes, submit one on my Challenges page. I'd love to try out my skills at
cooking something that I've never done before. I'm a firm believer that you'll never learn to get better unless you step out of
your comfort zone!
